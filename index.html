Why this one matters to me

I was born on June 28 — the very date of the first Pride march, in 1970 — and this year I turn 28 on the 28th: my golden birthday, landing exactly on that anniversary. I get to mark it in the city where I first saw two men kiss openly and understood what was possible for me as a gay man from Nigeria.

And it's the start of my own gold rush, starting the next chapter of my career in the mecca of the great American gold rush. I'd love you there for it.

— Arinze

Know before you go

A few helpful things

So your weekend is smooth from the moment you arrive.

🚇 Getting around

  • Take BART or Muni into the city Sunday — Market St. and Civic Center close for the parade.
  • Skip driving downtown; rideshare drop-offs get rerouted around closures.
  • Our Sunday spots are all transit-close to each other.

🎟️ Before the weekend

  • Buy day-party tickets early — Pride parties sell out.
  • Watch for my text with the brunch reservation details.
  • Screenshot your tickets; service gets patchy in crowds.

🧥 What to bring

  • Layers — warm afternoons, cold foggy nights (very SF).
  • Comfy shoes for standing and dancing.
  • Sunscreen, water, a portable charger.

🌈 The vibe

  • Wear color — gold for the golden birthday gets bonus points.
  • Come as you are; everyone's welcome at every part.
  • Mostly: just show up and celebrate with me.
